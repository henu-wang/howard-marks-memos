## Who Is Howard Marks?

Howard Marks, born in 1946, is the co-founder and co-chairman of Oaktree Capital Management, one of the world's largest investors in distressed debt with over $170 billion in assets under management. He is widely regarded as one of the most insightful investment thinkers alive.

Since 1990, Marks has written regular memos to Oaktree's clients that have become essential reading for investors worldwide. Warren Buffett has said, "When I see memos from Howard Marks in my mail, they're the first thing I open and read." These memos offer deep analysis of market psychology, risk, cycles, and investor behavior.

## Core Concepts

### Second-Level Thinking

Marks's most important concept. First-level thinking is simplistic and superficial: "This is a good company, let's buy the stock." Second-level thinking is deep and complex: "This is a good company, but everyone thinks it's a great company, and the stock is overpriced. Let's sell."

Superior investment results require thinking differently and better than the consensus. If your thinking is the same as everyone else's, you will get the same results as everyone else.

*"First-level thinking says, 'It's a good company; let's buy the stock.' Second-level thinking says, 'It's a good company, but everyone thinks it's a great company, and it's not. So the stock's overrated, and let's sell.'"*

### Understanding Risk

Marks argues that risk is not the same as volatility. True investment risk is the probability of permanent capital loss. The riskiest moment in markets is when everyone believes risk is low, because that belief drives prices to unsustainable levels.

*"The biggest risk is not the risk of losing money. It's the risk of missing opportunity."*

Key risk insights:
- Risk cannot be measured precisely; it can only be judged
- Risk increases as prices rise, not as they fall
- Risk is highest when the consensus believes risk is lowest
- The riskiest thing is buying assets at prices that leave no room for error

### Market Cycles

Marks believes that understanding market cycles is the single most important skill an investor can develop. Markets oscillate between euphoria and panic, greed and fear. These cycles are driven by human psychology and are largely predictable in their patterns, though not in their timing.

The cycle has three stages:
1. **Recovery** — Smart money buys when things look worst
2. **Boom** — Optimism grows, prices rise, risk increases
3. **Bust** — Excess is corrected, prices fall, opportunities emerge

*"You can't predict. You can prepare."*

### The Pendulum of Investor Psychology

Marks describes investor sentiment as a pendulum that swings between two extremes:
- Euphoria and depression
- Greed and fear
- Risk tolerance and risk aversion
- Credulity and skepticism

The pendulum spends very little time at the midpoint. Understanding where the pendulum is at any given moment is essential for making good investment decisions.

### Contrarian Thinking

Superior returns come from going against the crowd at the right times. This requires both analytical skill and emotional courage. It is not enough to simply be contrarian — you must be contrarian and correct.

*"To buy when others are despondently selling and to sell when others are euphorically buying takes the greatest courage, but provides the greatest profit."*

## Notable Quotes

- *"Experience is what you got when you didn't get what you wanted."*
- *"Being too far ahead of your time is indistinguishable from being wrong."*
- *"There's a big difference between probability and outcome."*
- *"The most dangerous thing is to think there's no risk."*
- *"Investment success doesn't come from buying good things, but rather from buying things well."*

## How to Apply Marks's Thinking

1. **Practice second-level thinking** — Always ask: "What does the consensus think, and why might it be wrong?"
2. **Respect cycles** — Study market history to recognize where you are in the current cycle.
3. **Focus on risk control** — Superior investors are defined by their ability to manage risk, not just by their returns.
4. **Be patient and contrarian** — The best opportunities come when others are panicking.
5. **Read the memos** — Marks's client memos are freely available on the Oaktree website and offer ongoing education.
